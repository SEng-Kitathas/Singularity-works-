# CROSS-LAB MERGE PROTOCOL — PCMMAD

## PURPOSE
Define how outputs from parallel Labs are reconciled into one stronger authority product.

## MERGE PRECONDITIONS
A merge SHALL NOT occur unless:
- targets are compatible
- authority bases are known
- artifact states are declared
- conflicts are surfaced explicitly

## MERGE TYPES
1. REINFORCING MERGE
- Labs agree and strengthen the same structure

2. COMPLEMENTARY MERGE
- Labs solve different parts of the same target

3. ADVERSARIAL MERGE
- One Lab exposes weaknesses in another and the result integrates the correction

## MERGE STEPS
1. Compare Labs using LAB COMPARISON PROTOCOL
2. Score candidate artifacts
3. Identify:
   - shared truths
   - contradictions
   - superior embodiments
   - dead branches
4. Produce one merge artifact with explicit lineage
5. Mark inputs as:
   - merged
   - held
   - superseded
   - killed

## RULES
- Do not merge unresolved contradictions silently
- Do not blend artifacts just to preserve effort
- Prefer the more real artifact over the more elegant narrative
- Preserve lineage so future Labs can audit the merge

## RESULT
The merge output becomes the new authority candidate for future sessions.
